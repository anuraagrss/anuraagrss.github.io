تو زندہ ہو تم

I grew up in a small town in India.

Our world was the street, the school, the neighbours.

"I thought that was the whole world."

Then I left. And I never stopped leaving.

Every country was a classroom. Every stranger, a mirror.

TOH ZINDA HO TUM.

Growing up,our world fit inside one town. I watched planes from rooftops and wondered where they were going. I didn't know then that the sky was an invitation — and that the most important education happens when you sit across from someone whose life looks nothing like yours, and realise you are the same.
— Anuraag Ravulaparthi
